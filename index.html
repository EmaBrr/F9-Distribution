New Micro Drones Arrive for Lithuanian Armed Forces

The Lithuanian Armed Forces have received the first Parrot Anafi UKR micro drones, part of a EUR 2.5 million procurement contract signed with Deftools UAB. These drones, designed for electronic warfare, are part of a broader EUR 36 million initiative to enhance the military's UAV capabilities. The acquisitions, including drones from Latvian and Estonian companies, are set to strengthen surveillance and operational capacity, with most deliveries expected by autumn 2025. Approximately EUR 200 million is planned for drone procurements through 2030 to support the development of modern armed forces.
